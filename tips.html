Other tips

  • The best places to meet freinds are electives (especially if you take art or drama) and clubs, especially hobby or interest-related ones.
  • Be polite to your teachers, don't disrupt class, and get as many assignments as possibe in on time, and unless you and your teachers really don't get along they'll probably let you get away with more than your less well-behaved classmates.
  • If you take public transportation, get to your stop earlier than you think you need to. Buses and trains fill up more the closer you get to the start of the school day.
  • Check your email often. Check your grades often. Set up email notifications on aspen.
  • Stay on the right side of hallways and stairwells whenever possible if they are crowded, which they almost always are.
  • You are not going to get along with everyone. Try no to make people too angry at you.
  • Try to focus on making genuinely good friends instead of finding a partner.
  • Take progressively more difficult/high level classes as you go; it looks good on college applications.
  • If you have accommodations, know what they are, use them, and advocate for yourself.
  • The menstrual products in the restrooms rarely get restocked. If you think you're going to need them, bring your own.
  • Taking good notes or at least paying attention in class to the best of your ability will minimize the need to study for tests.
  • You can remove up to 4 demerits by setting a goal attendance week with this handy link.
  • Have fun!

Tips

  • Different hallways are more or less active at different times of day. Try to avoid crowded hallways and stairwells.
  • Consult a map before the school year begins and try to locate all of your classrooms, and try to find the best routes between them.
  • PE class locations are posted on a board outside the 1st floor locker rooms, and are usually accurate.
  • Your locker will likely be near your advisory classroom.
  • Unless you are taking advanced classes, it is unlikely that you will have any classes except for your advisory classroom or a class like ceramics that requires a studio.
  • Techers have different times when they consider you tardy. If you get to class within 5 minutes after the bell and don't have a strict teacher, you will probably be fine, especially if you have PE or a class on the opposite side of the building.
  • You will probably get lost at at least one point, but there will almost certainly be other people around to ask for directions. Try and find the person most likely to know where the room you're looking for is.
